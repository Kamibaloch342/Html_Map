Migration & Border Strain

Afghan Migration (1979-Present)

Reason

Over 3.7 million Afghans live in Pakistan, including 1.4 million registered refugees and over 1.3 million undocumented migrants. Post-August 2021 Taliban takeover led to a surge in illegal crossings.

Economic Impact

  • Strain on Public Services: Afghan refugees overcrowd healthcare and education systems: hospitals in urban centres operate at 175% capacity, and classroom congestion has worsened. Hosting 700,000 new arrivals is estimated to cost $2.2 billion over three years.
  • Labor Market Impact & Wage Suppression: Afghan migrants fill the informal labor market, often accepting wages lower than local workers—this suppresses local wages and increases unemployment among Pakistanis.
  • Revenue Loss & Smuggling-Linked Costs: Many Afghan traders operate unofficially, eroding tax revenue. Illicit economies thrive in border regions; fuel and currency smuggling cost Pakistan $3.3 billion annually.

Hostilities with India

Kargil War (1999)

Reason

A covert infiltration of Pakistani soldiers and paramilitary forces—disguised as Kashmiri militants—into strategic positions on the Indian side of the Line of Control (LoC).

Comment & Implications

The conflict placed immense strain on Pakistan’s fiscal resources, diverting public funds from development to defence and causing a noticeable slowdown in economic activity. The war deeply undermined investor confidence, leading to a decline in investment and weakening prospects for long-term economic growth and private sector development. It aggravated existing structural weaknesses in the economy, leaving Pakistan more exposed to future shocks.

Financial & Regulatory Pressure

FATF Grey Listing (2008-2022)

Reason

  • Weak anti-money laundering (AML) & counter-terror financing (CFT) regulations.
  • Lack of compliance in freezing assets of terror outfits and regulating charities.
  • Inadequate actions against terror financing, especially linked to LeT, JeM, and others.

Comment & Implications

This isn't just economic loss—reduced consumption, shrinking exports, and collapsed investment erode public welfare and can become security threats, fueling social unrest or extremist narratives. Greylisting also puts Pakistan in a tough position geopolitically, locking in economic vulnerability and affecting long-term developmental priorities.

Migration & Border Strain

Illegal Migration via Turkey (2015-Present)

Reason

Thousands of Pakistanis attempting illegal migration to Europe via Turkey, who began mass deportations since 2018. In 2022 alone, over 13,000 Pakistanis were deported, many returning with no reintegration plan.

Economic Impact

  • High Smuggling Revenue: Human trafficking networks generate about Rs 40–50 billion annually for criminal syndicates.
  • Large-Scale Illicit Transfers: The "dunki" route costs around $11,926 per person, implying total illicit transfers of over US$300 million yearly.
  • Remittance Leakage: Irregular migration deprives Pakistan of formal remittance inflows, fueling fiscal instability.

Comment & Implications

The large-scale movement of migrants, coupled with trafficking revenue, erodes state authority at borders and fosters corruption, weakening economic and security structures.

Internal Security

CPEC Insurgency (2015-2025)

Reason

A growing wave of armed resistance and militant attacks targeting the China–Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC), particularly in Balochistan and parts of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. It’s driven by a mix of ethno-nationalist grievances, resource exploitation concerns, and geopolitical tensions.

Comment & Implications

The cost of securing CPEC is substantial. Already fragile finances are strained by direct security spending, consumer impacts via electricity tariff surcharges, and project delays. These pressures threaten Pakistan’s economic objectives under CPEC and weaken strategic autonomy, as resources are redirected from development to defence.

Internal Economic Stress

Debt & IMF Conditionality (FY 2025-26)

Economic Impact

Pakistan’s public debt stands at approximately PKR 76 trillion. A large portion of government revenue is tied up in debt servicing, leaving insufficient funds for essential sectors. IMF-mandated reforms curb domestic demand, slowing down recovery and job creation.

Implications for National Security

Austerity measures heighten public dissatisfaction, creating conditions for unrest. IMF conditionalities narrow Pakistan’s policy choices, impacting autonomy in critical geopolitical decisions.

Geopolitical & External Shocks

US Global Trade War (Projected 2025)

Economic Impact on Pakistan

  • Projected Export Losses: A 29% U.S. tariff threat could slash exports by 20–25%, amounting to annual losses of $1.1–1.4 billion. Textiles are most vulnerable, risking half a million jobs.
  • Trade Deficit & Currency Pressures: Export declines may widen the current account deficit, weaken the rupee, and fuel inflation.

National Security Implications

Economic instability could undermine social cohesion and elevate risks of internal unrest. Diplomatic strain would limit options for economic diversification.
